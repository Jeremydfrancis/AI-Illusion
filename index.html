The Global Workforce Is Under Threat

The Illusion of AI

What was once an assistant to human decision-making has quickly become a replacement for actual humans, and the consequences are only beginning to surface.

91% of organizations say entry-level roles are either significantly changing or entirely disappearing

The Problem

Organizations Are Over-Trusting Inaccurate Technology

AI gathers an immense amount of information within seconds but identifying the accuracy of the information AI provides is challenging. AI's inability to reliably provide accurate information leaves organizations vulnerable and facing serious ramifications.

The Evidence

Three Ways AI Is Failing The Workforce

Modern AI facilitates more harm than good because organizations are too dependent on inaccurate technology, it eliminates entry-level positions, and it disrupts the pipeline of talented individuals needed for long-term success.

Over-Reliance

The False Sense of Credibility AI Creates

A global study conducted by the University of Melbourne and KPMG, surveying over 48,000 people across 47 countries, found that 66% of people rely on AI output without challenging its accuracy, and 56% report making mistakes in their work as a direct result. This high level of trust becomes risky, especially in professional fields where accuracy is mandatory.

Gillespie et al., University of Melbourne & KPMG, 2025

Over-Reliance

Less experienced pathologists followed inaccurate AI results even when they previously diagnosed the correct results without the AI's assistance. The false sense of credibility AI creates can persuade even the most experienced individuals to second guess their own judgment.

Job Elimination

Junior-level job postings have declined 35% since January 2023, with 66% of enterprises actively slowing down on hiring junior roles. 91% of organizations acknowledge these roles are either significantly changing or entirely disappearing.

Job Elimination

Removing These Roles Is a Recipe for Disaster

Companies are replacing entry positions with AI not because those workers could not complete the tasks but because they confidently believe AI can perform the routine tasks at a lower short-term cost. Removing these roles without acknowledging what is lost in the transaction is a recipe for disaster. 37% of managers prefer to integrate AI tools than hire an entry-level employee.

Woollacott / IDC, 2025. Bantock & SignalFire, 2025

Pipeline Destruction

A Company Running on Borrowed Time

The elimination of entry-level roles not only harms the workers who lose these roles; it dismantles the pipeline of experienced professionals that organizations depend on in the future. Without an entry-level position leading into mid-level positions, organizations will quickly deplete their pools of mid-level talent. 71% of organizations already report difficulty developing future leaders because of the positions being cut.

Woollacott / IDC, 2025. Ly et al., 2026

Pipeline Destroyed

Without having entry-level experience, applicants lack the advanced skills required for the remaining roles. A company that completely disregards the entry-level role is a company that is running on borrowed time.

The Truth

What Advocates Say & The Actual Data

The promise of net job growth and retraining programs obscures reality until the data behind those promises is examined.

01

Their claim

AI is transforming the job market by generating new roles. WEF projects 170 million new roles created by 2030 with a net gain of 78 million jobs globally.

The reality

The workers being displaced are unlikely to be the same workers who fill these new roles. Demand for AI is mainly concentrated in professional, technical, and managerial occupations. Displaced workers in high-exposure, low-skill roles face lower employment outcomes as AI spreads. Most critically, the same WEF report discloses that over 41% of companies are reducing their overall workforce now, not in 2030.

Jaumotte et al., IMF 2026. World Economic Forum, 2025

02

Their claim

The disruption caused by AI is being actively managed through investment in upskilling. Two-thirds of enterprises are already investing in AI training programs.

The reality

Only 3% of companies have a coordinated upskilling program and 29% are not aware of who can train these workers. 57% encounter limited employee engagement, 51% struggle with budget constraints, and 45% cannot find qualified trainers. The displaced workers are being told "help is on the way," but the data plainly reveals this is not accurate.

Woollacott / IDC, 2025

What Must Change

Why It Matters

Modern use of AI is not only a technological shift but a structural transformation to the global workforce. When companies blindly trust unreliable technology, eliminate foundational roles, and displace workers without a sustainable path forward, the global consequences will be dire.

Society cannot afford to continue believing that technological progress and human progress run in parallel to each other. If the current path continues, job seekers will find themselves competing for jobs they were never prepared to do. AI is here to stay, but organizations need to take responsibility for how they use it within the modern workforce before it is too late.
